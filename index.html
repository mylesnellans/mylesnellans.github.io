About

My name is Myles Nellans. I am currently a junior at the univeirsty of Tennessee Knoxville studying Wildlife and Fisheries Science. In addition to this I am a undergraduate researcher in the Sheldon Lab in the Ecology and Evolutionary Biology Department. My research explores how envirmental factors affect ectotherms and ho wthey may adapt. I am partiulary interested in the effect global change has on teh reproductive success of insects.

I have also been fortunate with many differnt species in different capasities. Some projects I have been involved in include population assesment, assesment of IPR system's on water quality, and prediction of future climate change on ectotherms.

I regulary update this webiste with my current work, conferences, and published articles. Please feel free to contact me with any questions

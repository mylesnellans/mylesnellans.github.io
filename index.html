About Me

Currently, I am an Undergraduate Researcher in the Sheldon Lab at the Univeristy of Tennessee Knoxville. Recetly, my focus has been assessing the thermal limits and fecundity of different Lepidoptera species that are accimated to differnt moisture and heat levels. I am broadly interested in ecology, ecophysiology, entomology, Herpetology, and global change. I am esspecially passionaite about assessing how different species will be affected by climate chnage in the future, and more importantly how they adapt. Over time, I have had the privilage to work with many differnt speices, inculding butterflies, dung beetles, fish, and salamanders!

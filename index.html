About

My name is Myles Nellans. I am currently a junior at the University of Tennessee Knoxville studying Wildlife and Fisheries Science. I also work as an undergraduate researcher in the Sheldon Lab where I investigate how temperature and moisture levels affect the Nymphalidae species resilience to environmental change. My research explores how environmental factors affect ectotherms and their ability to adapt. I am particularly interested in the effect global change has on the reproductive success of insects.

I have also been fortunate enough to work with many different species across different capacities. Some projects I have been involved in include population assessment, evaluation of IPR systems on water quality, and prediction of future climate change impacts on ectotherms.

I regularly update this website with my current work, conferences, and published articles. Please feel free to contact me with any questions.
